The Data Commons Graph (DCG) is constructed by synthesizing a single Graph from many different data sources. It currently contains data from Wikipedia, the US Census, NOAA, FBI, etc. The browser can be used both to explore what data is available and to understand the graph structure, to help use the API.

You can use the search box above or start looking around from some well known starting points:

  • Cities: Pittsburgh, PA, Mountain View, CA, Austin, TX, Berkeley, CA, More ...
  • Counties: Santa Clara, Miami-Dade, More ...
  • States: Florida, California, Wyoming, New York, More ...
  • Countries: United States, Canada, More ...
  • Congressional Districts: CA-18, NY-01, More ...
  • Elections: 2016 US Presidential Election, 1990 US Senate Elections for Michigan, More ...
  • Census Estimates : Asian Population in Santa Clara, Master Degree Holders in New Orleans
  • Labor Statistics : Unemployment in Boston
  • Crime Data: Violent Robbery in Atlanta, Georgia, Larceny-Theft in Georgia
  • Health Data: Prevalence of Diabetes in Atlanta
  • DEA ARCOS: Cocaine distribution in Palm Beach, FL, Amphetamine distribution in Wayne County, WV
  • Biological Specimens: Austrobaileya scandens, More ...
  • Power Plants: APS West Phoenix, More ...
  • ENCODE (Encyclopedia Of DNA Elements): Labs, Biosamples, Experiments, More ...
  • Public Schools and School Districts: Henry M. Gunn High, Stuyvesant High School, New York City Department of Education
